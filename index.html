Internet as Food

The internet is a buffet. At its core, the internet feeds us. We arrive hungry — hungry for knowledge, distraction, validation, entertainment. We scroll through endless platters of content, sampling bites until something satisfies our craving. Some days we snack. Other days we binge.

Users are diners. We sit at glowing tables, menus curated by algorithms that learn our tastes. We accept cookies without reading the recipe, leaving crumbs of data behind with every click.

The web is both pantry and marketplace. For those with access, it’s always open — a 24/7 drive-thru of information.

The tunnels connect like kitchen corridors. What feels like a single website is really a doorway into a sprawling food court.
